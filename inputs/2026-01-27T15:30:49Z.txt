## Summary
This lecture provides training on the Kentucky vehicle titling system (KYELT/VTAS) and its integration with the state‚Äôs system of record, CAVIS. The instructor explains workflow differences between Kentucky and other states (like West Virginia), emphasizing that VTAS is not the system of record in Kentucky. Key topics include distinguishing transaction types (Transfer, Dealer Assignment, Transfer with Assignment), the evolution from manual data entry to integrated workflows, and the role of legacy systems like the Mainframe. Future plans cover ELT implementation, Gen 2 upgrades, and AI verification (Cade), along with the revenue model based on transaction fees.
## Knowledge Points
### 1. System Architecture and Workflow
* **Kentucky System Components**
   - **V2 (VATU)**: External dealer-facing interface submitting transaction data via API; entry point for data.
   - **VTAS (KYELT)**: Internal ‚ÄúGen 1‚Äù system that receives V2 data. Despite the KYELT name, it handles more than Electronic Lien and Title.
   - **CAVIS**: Official System of Record for Kentucky. Unlike some states (e.g., WV) where the company‚Äôs software is the system of record, Kentucky requires data to be finalized in CAVIS.
* **Legacy Mainframe Usage**
   - **Migration**: The state still uses an old Mainframe. When processing older titles, clerks may need to migrate data from the Mainframe to create a record in CAVIS.
   - **‚ÄúPipe Stem‚Äù Search**: The Mainframe is used for specific searches (‚Äúpipe stem‚Äù) because the new system allows title aliasing, which the state worries could be exploited (e.g., gang members tracking police vehicles).
* **Integration and Data Entry**
   - **Manual Process**: Previously, clerks reviewed a transfer in VTAS, then items moved to ‚ÄúCAVIS Action Required.‚Äù Clerks opened CAVIS separately, copied/pasted every field, and entered the new title number back into VTAS to complete.
   - **Integrated Process**: Now items move to ‚ÄúCAVIS In Progress.‚Äù Data is pre-populated in CAVIS, reducing manual copy-paste. Clerks still verify data and may manually enter fields like ‚ÄúLien Issue Date.‚Äù
### 2. Transaction Types and Definitions
* **Transfers (New vs. Used)**
   - **New Vehicles**: Dealers selling brand-new vehicles can transfer directly to the customer using an MSO; no need to title in the dealer‚Äôs name first.
   - **Used Vehicles**: Dealers generally must assign the vehicle to themselves (title in dealer‚Äôs name) before selling to the final customer.
* **Dealer Assignment**
   - Assigns a vehicle from an owner to a dealer; the dealer takes ownership (title in their name). Fees and taxes apply, and the DMV requires reporting to collect revenue.
* **Transfer with Assignment**
   - **Context**: Historically, dealers could sign the back of a title to transfer from Dealer A ‚Üí Dealer B ‚Üí Dealer C without notifying the DMV, causing missed fees.
   - **Process**: Captures the full chain: Owner A transfers to the Dealer, then the Dealer immediately sells to Owner B (Buyer). All steps are submitted in one application. If multiple dealers are involved, multiple assignments are recorded so the DMV can track and collect taxes.
### 3. Verification, Future Plans, and Business Model
* **Verification Processes**
   - **Manual Review**: Kentucky clerks heavily double-check data against scanned evidence (title image) and NMVTIS.
   - **Cade (AI) Integration**: Currently used in West Virginia for 2-way comparison. Goal in Kentucky is 3-way comparison: System Data vs. OCR from Scanned Evidence vs. NMVTIS.
* **Future Roadmaps (Gen 1 vs. Gen 2)**
   - **Gen 1**: Current KYELT/VTAS combining front-end and back-end functions.
   - **Gen 2**: New headless back-end requiring a separate portal (e.g., 360 portal).
   - **Plans**: ELT (Electronic Lien and Title)/Lanes will be added later this year; discussion ongoing about upgrading Kentucky to Gen 2 at the same time.
